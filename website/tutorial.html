JPSTrack Tutorial

Thank you for downloading JPSTrack, the little GPX tracker with attitude! If you are new to the app, note that it is not a traditional "navigation app", but is a map-maker's tool intended to be most useful for those contributing the the Open Street Map, "the free, editable map of the world."

Safety Notice

Using jpstrack

Using the app is actually pretty simple. There are three main buttons in one row

Startstarts recording a GPX track
Pause/Resumepauses and resumes
StopCloses the file.

Note that when you start recording, the actual filename is displayed to the right of the Start button.

A second row of buttons controls the adjunct functions:

Voice NoteStart dictating a note.
Text NoteStart typing a note.
Take PictureTake a snapshot (not currently available on BlackBerry Playbook).

The log window appears below this row of buttons on small-screen devices, and in the right half of the screen on devices like tablets.

Uploading Data

To have your traces uploaded automatically when you complete them, just enable this feature in Settings, and enter your OSM password. You will be prompted for your OSM password when you press Stop to end a recording.

If you don't have an OSM account, you should probably Sign Up for an OSM account sometime soon while you are getting started with mapping.

If the auto-upload fails, or you just would rather, you can also manually upload your data. Or you may just want to import your data into JOSM on your desktop. In either case, connect your Android Device to your computer, mount it as a USB device, and use a program like JOSM to upload the GPX files.

A File Manager feature is planned for jpstrack to make on-demand uploading simpler.
